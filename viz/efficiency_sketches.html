Boston's Offensive Efficiency: Visualization Sketches

Exploring how to show that Boston has great team offensive efficiency despite not having elite individual efficiency — through broad positive contributions and zero negative contributors.

1. Dot Plot with Minutes as Size (Your Original Idea)

Each dot is a player, size = minutes played, y = offensive rating. Teams sorted by floor (worst player's rating).

2. Strip Plot (Minutes as opacity)

Same data but using opacity for minutes. Horizontal line at league average (112).

3. Small Multiples: Distribution per Team

Each team gets its own column showing the distribution of player offensive ratings.

4. Stacked Bar: Minutes by Efficiency Tier

What percentage of each team's minutes go to negative (<110), neutral (110-115), and positive (>115) contributors?

5. Beeswarm-ish: Offset by Minutes Rank

Players offset horizontally by minutes rank within team (1st = leftmost), y = off rating. Shows depth clearly.

6. Floor vs Ceiling Scatter

Each team as a point: x = worst player's rating (floor), y = best player's rating (ceiling). Boston has highest floor.

7. Cumulative Minutes by Efficiency

For each team, show cumulative % of minutes as you go from worst to best offensive rating. Steeper = more minutes to better players.
